# Simple Semantics 

**Simple Semantics** is a minimal, text-driven decision game about meaning, interpretation, and responsibility.  
You do not speak directly to the world — you rule through interpretation.

In this game, *words matter*, but not always in the way you expect.

---

## > How the Game Works

You play as **the Queen**.

Non-player characters (NPCs) bring you messages — requests, prophecies, warnings, or advice.  
These messages are **not instructions**. They are *open to interpretation*.

The system works on a simple but tricky idea:

- NPCs **send messages**
- You **interpret** those messages
- The **Queen takes action**
- Coins are rewarded based on *interpretation logic*, not moral correctness

Even if:
- The message was misunderstood X  
- The action taken was not what the NPC “meant” X  

You may **still gain coins**, because:
- The Queen acted decisively  
- Or the fault lies in interpretation, not intention  

Likewise:
- You may correctly understand a message  
- But the Queen’s action may still lead elsewhere  

Meaning is unstable. Authority is final.

---

## 🎮 How to Play

1. Read the message presented by an NPC.
2. Interpret what *you think* it means.
3. Make a decision **as the Queen**, not as yourself.
4. Observe the outcome.
5. Gain coins — not because you were right, but because you ruled.

There is **no single correct path**.  
The game rewards interpretation, not obedience.

---

## 🪙 About Coins & Decisions

- Coins are **not a morality score**
- Coins are tied to **decisions made by the Queen**
- Correct interpretation ≠ correct outcome
- Incorrect interpretation ≠ failure

The Queen’s authority stands even when meaning breaks.
